City Gallery

Herat

Herat is a historic, culturally rich city in western of Afghanistan, known as the "Pearl of Khorasan" and a Key Silk Road Hub, Famed for its stunning Islamic architecture like the Herat Citadel and Friday Mosque, flourishing arts, and strategic importance as a crossroads for trade, historically linked to Persian and Timurid empires, though it faces modern challenges with urbanization and preservation. .

Kandahar

Kandahar is the second-largest city in Afghanistan and the capital of Kandahar Province, serving as the nation's spiritual and traditional Pashtun heartland. Located in the south between the Arghandab and Tarnak rivers, it is a major agricultural, trade, and economic hub known for exporting fruit. With a history dating back to 3000 BC and founded as Alexandria in the 4th century BC, it has been a crucial strategic, political, and military center for centuries.

Mazar-i-Sharif

Mazar-i-Sharif, the capital of Balkh province, is a major, relatively liberal commercial hub in northern Afghanistan with a population of over 485,000. Famous for the stunning Blue Mosque (Shrine of Hazrat Ali), it serves as a key trade, agriculture, and transit point for Central Asian goods.

Bamyan

Bamyan, known as the "Valley of Gods" and "Shining Light," is a historically rich city in central Afghanistan’s Hindu Kush mountains, famously home to the destroyed 5th-century giant Buddha statues. As a former Silk Road hub and Buddhist center, it is a UNESCO World Heritage site featuring thousands of ancient caves, the 12th-century City of Screams (Shahr-e Gholghola), and proximity to Band-e Amir National Park.

Maidan Wardak

Maidan Wardak province, located in central Afghanistan, is primarily famous for its high-altitude mountainous landscapes—notably the Shah Foladi peak—rich agricultural production, especially apples in the Jaghatu district, and its strategic location on the highway connecting Kabul to the Hazarajat region. It is also known for numerous ancient historical sites, including castles, stupas, and shrines.

Khost

Khost is the largest city in southeastern Afghanistan and the capital of Khost Province, is primarily famous for its strategic border location, its "cradle of cricket" reputation, and a history of fierce tribal independence and famous dry is pine nuts which makes it globaly famous.

Nengarhar

Nangarhar is widely known as the "garden province" of Afghanistan due to its fertile valleys and warm climate, which make it the country's agricultural heartland.

Ghazni

Ghazni a historic city in southeastern Afghanistan, is most famous for being the seat of the Ghaznavid Empireand a former "Islamic Capital of Culture.

City of the Day

Kabul

Kabul city is divided into 22 municipal districts, commonly referred to as Police Districts (PDs) or nahia, which cover the urban area, while the surrounding Kabul Province consists of 145 or more administrative districts (woluswalis), including Paghman, Bagrami, and Surobi. The city districts are responsible for local administration and urban management.
